# Seven Organizational Tensions of the AI Era
## Deep Research Brief — March 2026

---

## 1. COMPRESSION VS. CAPABILITY BUILDING

**The tension:** AI lets you run lean, but lean can become hollow. Where is the line between efficient compression and dangerous hollowing?

### Companies Compressing Successfully

**Shopify (Tobi Lutke)** — In April 2025, Lutke published an internal memo (which he then posted publicly) declaring: "Reflexive AI usage is now a baseline expectation at Shopify." The key mandate: "Before asking for more headcount and resources, teams must demonstrate why they cannot get what they want done using AI." AI competency is now a formal part of performance reviews and hiring decisions. Shopify has not reported the mass quality failures seen at other companies — possibly because the mandate was about *augmentation* (prove AI can't do it before hiring) rather than *replacement* (fire people and let AI try).
- Source: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/04/07/shopify-ceo-tells-teams-to-consider-using-ai-before-growing-headcount/); [Tobi Lutke on X](https://x.com/tobi/status/1909251946235437514)

**Coinbase (Brian Armstrong)** — Armstrong gave engineers one week to adopt AI coding assistants (Copilot and Cursor) or face termination. By mid-2025, ~40% of daily code at Coinbase was AI-generated, with a target of >50%. In one session, over 500 pull requests went up in 15 minutes. The company uses DRIs (Directly Responsible Individuals) to maintain accountability while pushing speed. Armstrong's approach is compression with *specificity* — he isn't cutting roles, he's amplifying individuals.
- Source: [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/08/25/coinbase-ceo-brian-armstrong-ai-coding-assistants-mandate-tech/); [The Block](https://www.theblock.co/post/369460/brian-armstrong-coinbase-ai)

**Midjourney (David Holz)** — Reached ~$200M in annual revenue and a multi-billion-dollar valuation with fewer than 15 employees. The extreme example of compression-from-inception — the company was built to be small, not compressed after the fact.
- Source: [Entrepreneur Loop](https://entrepreneurloop.com/one-person-startup-billion-dollar-company-of-one/)

### Companies That Compressed Too Far

**Klarna (Sebastian Siemiatkowski)** — The highest-profile cautionary tale. Klarna reduced headcount from 5,527 to ~3,422 (40%) primarily through attrition after a hiring freeze. Its AI chatbot handled 2.3 million conversations in its first month, doing the work of 700 full-time customer service agents. Revenue grew 108% over three years. Remaining employee pay rose from $126K to $203K. It looked like a triumph — until it wasn't.

Siemiatkowski publicly admitted: **"We went too far... We focused too much on efficiency and cost. The result was lower quality, and that's not sustainable."** Klarna is now rehiring human staff after customer complaints about service quality.
- Source: [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/05/14/klarna-ceo-says-ai-helped-company-shrink-workforce-by-40percent.html); [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91468582/klarna-tried-to-replace-its-workforce-with-ai); [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/05/09/klarna-ai-humans-return-on-investment/)

**Duolingo (Luis von Ahn)** — Cut ~10% of contractor workforce (translators, then writers) in late 2023 and 2024, replacing them with AI content generation. Von Ahn stated: **"We'd rather move with urgency and take occasional small hits on quality than move slowly and miss the moment."** Users complained content felt "repetitive, robotic, or lacked the playful tone that made Duolingo iconic." Gross margins actually *dropped* (73.0% to 71.1%) due to AI compute costs. Consumers threatened boycotts. Von Ahn later admitted his AI-first memo "did not give enough context."
- Source: [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2025/05/04/is-duolingo-the-face-of-an-ai-jobs-crisis/); [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/06/09/duolingo-ceo-surprised-backlash-ai-first-company-announcement/)

### Surprise / Counterintuitive Finding

Klarna's compression initially *looked* successful by every financial metric. Revenue up 108%. Pay up 60%. Headcount down 40%. The failure was invisible — it was in *quality* and *trust*, which are lagging indicators. The lesson: **financial metrics are leading indicators of compression success but lagging indicators of capability loss.** By the time the numbers show the problem, the institutional knowledge is already gone.

Also counterintuitive: Duolingo's AI-first move actually *increased* costs (compute) while *decreasing* quality. The assumption that AI replacement = cost savings is not always true.

### How Might We...

- **HMW** distinguish between "fat" that can safely be compressed and "muscle" that looks like fat but is load-bearing institutional capability?
- **HMW** create leading indicators for capability loss before it shows up in customer satisfaction or financial metrics?
- **HMW** help leaders set a "compression floor" — the minimum human capability below which the organization becomes brittle?

## 2. AUTONOMY VS. COHERENCE

**The tension:** AI pushes decision-making power to the edges. But distributed power without shared context creates chaos.

### Radical Autonomy Working

**McKinsey's "Agentic Organization" Model** — McKinsey's 2025 research describes a new paradigm: small teams of 2-5 people supervising 50-100 specialized AI agents running end-to-end processes. The key: "Winning operating models will empower agentic teams with flat decision and communication structures that operate with high context sharing and alignment across teams." Organization charts pivot from hierarchy to "agentic networks or work charts based on exchanging tasks and outcomes." One manager with a small human team could orchestrate hundreds of agents running 24/7.
- Source: [McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/people-and-organizational-performance/our-insights/the-agentic-organization-contours-of-the-next-paradigm-for-the-ai-era)

**Coinbase's DRI Model** — Uses "Directly Responsible Individuals" — a single-threaded leader for each initiative who balances input from the team with clear decision authority. This is autonomy with accountability: distributed power, but every decision has a named owner.
- Source: [Coinbase Blog](https://medium.com/the-coinbase-blog/operating-efficiently-at-scale-e6e2378d3d4)

### Radical Autonomy Creating Chaos

**Valve Corporation** — The classic cautionary tale of flat organization. Valve operates without formal managers, with employees choosing their projects. But co-founder Gabe Newell has admitted the structure "fails to catch bad decisions early due to a lack of internal controls." Former employees describe a "pseudo-flat structure" with hidden hierarchy where personal relationships overshadow project merit. **The quality of hiring determines whether self-organization becomes innovation or chaos.**
- Source: [Agile Federation](https://www.agilefederation.com/post/valve-corporation-a-case-study-in-the-future-of-organizational-structure); [SSRN](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3884844)

**Lattice's "Digital Worker" Debacle** — In July 2024, HR platform Lattice announced AI agents would be treated as employees on its platform — with employee records, managers, and performance reviews. The backlash was immediate and severe. Within 3 days they reversed course. The lesson: **autonomy requires shared ontology.** When the organization couldn't agree on what constituted a "worker," giving AI agents organizational autonomy was premature. CEO Sarah Franklin later said: "We sparked a very important conversation."
- Source: [SHRM](https://www.shrm.org/topics-tools/news/technology/lattice-scraps-plans-to-treat-ai-bots-as-employees-after-backlash); [Inc.](https://www.inc.com/ben-sherry/nevermind-hr-company-lattice-decided-to-bring-ais-into-org-chart-then-changed-its-mind.html)

### Military Parallels — Mission Command

**U.S. Army Mission Command Doctrine** — The military parallel is remarkably direct. The Army defines mission command as empowering subordinate decision-making based on "competence, mutual trust, shared understanding, commander's intent, mission-type orders, disciplined initiative, and risk acceptance." This is a framework for *coherent autonomy* — distributed execution within shared intent.

A March 2025 War on the Rocks article, "The U.S. Army, Artificial Intelligence, and Mission Command," describes AI as enabling "a fusion of human and machine judgment, enabling data-centric warfare." AI agents can "compress decision timelines and enable smaller, more resilient command posts" — shrinking the staff while making it more effective.
- Source: [War on the Rocks](https://warontherocks.com/2025/03/the-u-s-army-artificial-intelligence-and-mission-command/)

**CSIS Agentic Warfare Research** — CSIS examined three AI-enabled staff models (Networked, Relational, and Adaptive) and found the **Adaptive model** most effective: "Smaller, faster staffs generate better options more quickly than larger legacy organizations." But challenges remain in AI literacy among officers and cyber-resilient networks.
- Source: [CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/rethinking-napoleonic-staff)

**American University Research** — "AI is about to radically alter military command structures," with the shift from monolithic command nodes to distributed command and control in contested environments.
- Source: [American University SIS](https://www.american.edu/sis/news/20250818-ai-is-about-to-radically-alter-military-command-structures.cfm)

### Surprise / Counterintuitive Finding

The military — typically seen as the most hierarchical institution — is actually the thought leader on *coherent autonomy*. Mission command doctrine has been solving the autonomy-coherence tension for decades. **The corporate world is reinventing what the military already knows: you need "commander's intent" (strategic coherence) to make "disciplined initiative" (tactical autonomy) work.** The corporate equivalent of "commander's intent" is what McKinsey calls "high context sharing and alignment across agentic teams."

### How Might We...

- **HMW** create the organizational equivalent of "commander's intent" — a coherence mechanism that enables autonomy rather than constraining it?
- **HMW** distinguish between productive autonomy and mere fragmentation, before the chaos becomes visible?
- **HMW** scale the coordination mechanisms that work at 15-person companies (Midjourney) to organizations of thousands?

## 3. SPEED VS. JUDGMENT

**The tension:** AI-native companies ship incredibly fast. But speed without judgment creates damage that moves equally fast.

### Fast AND Wise

**Anthropic (Dario Amodei)** — The Responsible Scaling Policy (RSP) is an organizational structure *designed* for deliberate judgment at high speed. The RSP uses AI Safety Levels (ASL) modeled on biosafety levels — graduated standards requiring stricter security, red-teaming, and deployment controls as model capability increases. The approval chain is deliberately layered: internal assessment, executive approval from both CEO and Responsible Scaling Officer, and governance notification to Board and LTBT. **Anthropic is a speed-oriented AI company that has embedded structural judgment into its operating model.**
- Source: [Anthropic RSP v3.0](https://www.anthropic.com/news/responsible-scaling-policy-v3)

**Pace Layers Framework Applied to AI Organizations** — Stewart Brand's "pace layers" concept (from *The Clock of the Long Now*, 1999) is being applied to AI organizational design. The insight: "AI supercharges the Fashion and Commerce layers. It ignores the Governance and Culture layers unless you deliberately build and reinforce them." Practitioners argue: **"Your pace layer system becomes your substitute for organizational structure. If you don't intentionally design the slow layers, you don't have any."**
- Source: [Mark Allen](https://markallen.io/pace-layers-ai-product-development); [Long Now Foundation](https://longnow.org/seminars/02015/jan/27/pace-layers-thinking/)

### Fast and Breaking Things

**Google AI Overviews** — Google rushed AI Overviews into Search and immediately produced errors: recommending users add glue to pizza (from satirical Reddit content), suggesting eating rocks daily, and in 2025, confidently telling users the year was still 2024. The speed-judgment gap: **AI systems can't recognize satire, and the organizational decision to ship before solving this was a judgment failure, not a technology failure.**
- Source: [MIT Technology Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2024/05/31/1093019/why-are-googles-ai-overviews-results-so-bad/)

**AI Coding Tools — Speed at the Cost of Quality** — A 2025 study of Cursor AI usage in open-source projects found that speed gains came with quality concerns. Devin AI correctly fixed only 13.86% of real-world GitHub issues end-to-end. Cursor's 2.1 release in November 2025 corrupted chat histories and worktrees. The broader finding: "2025 was the year of AI speed. 2026 will be the year of AI quality."
- Source: [CodeRabbit](https://www.coderabbit.ai/blog/2025-was-the-year-of-ai-speed-2026-will-be-the-year-of-ai-quality); [Hacker News discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=47401734)

### Emerging Frameworks

**Productboard** argues: "Velocity stops being the differentiator. Judgment is." And: **"With speed at their fingertips, product teams are now constrained by how clearly they can define the problem they are trying to solve. Without that clarity, speed only accelerates misalignment."**
- Source: [Productboard](https://www.productboard.com/blog/product-craft-when-ai-changes-the-stakes/)

**Fast Company** frames it: **"The most important decision is not how much AI you deploy, but where you decide its authority ends."** There is a moment where complexity increases, emotion rises, and the question becomes about reassurance, judgment, or accountability — that moment is the real leadership test.
- Source: [Fast Company](https://www.fastcompany.com/91516892/human-judgment-in-a-world-of-ai-decisions)

### Surprise / Counterintuitive Finding

The "fast AND wise" exemplar is not a tech startup — it's a military-style governance framework (Anthropic's RSP). **The fastest-moving AI company in terms of capability development has also built the most structured judgment apparatus.** Speed and judgment are not on a spectrum; they can be orthogonal if you design for both. The key is *structural* judgment (baked into process) rather than *individual* judgment (dependent on the decision-maker in the moment).

### How Might We...

- **HMW** build organizational "pace layers" that let the fast layers move at AI speed while the slow layers (governance, culture, values) maintain stability?
- **HMW** create "judgment infrastructure" that scales with the speed of AI-assisted output?
- **HMW** distinguish between problems where speed is the bottleneck and problems where judgment is the bottleneck?

## 4. ROLES VS. FLOWS

**The tension:** AI-native organizations think in workflows, not job descriptions. But humans need identity, career paths, and legibility.

### Moving to Flow-Based Work

**Ravin Jesuthasan & John Boudreau — "Work Without Jobs"** — The foundational framework. Jesuthasan (Mercer) and Boudreau (USC) argue skills should be the primary unit of work rather than rigid job titles. "Only through a concerted effort that involves deconstructing, redeploying, and reconstructing work tasks will organizations be able to fundamentally redesign work processes." Their approach: decompose jobs into tasks, evaluate which tasks AI should do, identify where human judgment is needed, reconstruct around outcomes.
- Source: [MIT Sloan Management Review](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/article/work-without-jobs/); [Ravin Jesuthasan](https://www.ravinjesuthasan.com/)

**Josh Bersin — "Job Redesign Around AI"** — Bersin's five imperatives include redesigning work, jobs, and organizational models. He argues: **"Jobs are giving way to skills as the currency of work... This pivot from jobs to skills will require us to rethink everything we know about work."** He identified emerging tools: Gloat (job decomposition into projects, tasks, skills, and talent), Reejig (task analysis and organizational task benchmarking), and Draup (enterprise-wide benchmarking and workload analysis).
- Source: [Josh Bersin](https://joshbersin.com/2025/03/job-redesign-around-ai-work-intelligence-tools-arrive/)

**Reejig (Siobhan Savage, CEO)** — Launched "Work Architecture" in 2025, a platform to "replace outdated job architectures" and enable organizations to "continuously evolve how work is structured, managed, and deployed across both people and AI." Key insight from Savage: **"AI doesn't automate skills; it automates tasks."** Across clients, Reejig mapped over 44,000 tasks and 36,000 skills, surfacing ~40% average AI potential across work and identifying >$7.4B in value opportunities.
- Source: [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/reejig-launches-work-architecture-the-critical-infrastructure-for-the-ai-powered-workforce-302489593.html); [Reejig](https://www.reejig.com/)

**TechWolf** — Built a complete job architecture in 2 weeks using its own AI agent — a process that traditionally takes months.
- Source: [TechWolf](https://www.techwolf.ai/resources/blog/how-techwolf-built-a-job-architecture-using-its-own-ai-agent)

### The Workflow-First Argument

People Managing People (2025) makes the case: **"The smarter move is to redesign workflows first, then build the organization around what the work actually requires."** The argument: "When you introduce tools that can handle meaningful portions of knowledge work, the org chart stops being a useful starting point. Roles designed before anyone understood the workflow become containers for tasks that may no longer need a human."
- Source: [People Managing People](https://peoplemanagingpeople.com/hr-operations/ai-workflow-redesign/)

### Emerging Role Types

McKinsey identifies new profiles emerging in the agentic organization:
- **Agent orchestrators** — design and supervise agent workflows
- **Hybrid managers** — lead blended human-agent teams
- **AI coaches** — help employees integrate AI into daily work
- Source: [McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/people-and-organizational-performance/our-insights/the-agentic-organization-contours-of-the-next-paradigm-for-the-ai-era)

### Scale of Change

The World Economic Forum's 2025 Future of Jobs Report projects that by 2030, 92 million jobs will be eliminated but 170 million new roles created — a net gain of 78 million. The question is not *whether* roles change but whether organizations can manage the transition.
- Source: [WEF](https://www.weforum.org/stories/2026/02/workforce-transformation-ai-jobs/)

### Surprise / Counterintuitive Finding

The biggest barrier to flow-based work isn't technology or even management resistance — it's **HR systems.** Compensation, benefits, career ladders, performance reviews, and compliance frameworks are all built around static job descriptions. Moving to flows requires rebuilding HR infrastructure, which is why tools like Reejig, Gloat, and Draup are emerging. **The slowest-moving part of the organization (HR systems) is the binding constraint on the fastest-moving change (AI-driven work redesign).**

### How Might We...

- **HMW** give people career identity and progression when their "role" changes quarterly?
- **HMW** redesign compensation systems for flow-based work where output is variable and roles are fluid?
- **HMW** maintain professional development and mastery when the "profession" keeps shifting?

## 5. INTERNAL VS. EXTERNAL

**The tension:** AI collapses the firm boundary. If transaction costs approach zero, why have a firm at all?

### Tiny Core, External Orchestration

**The One-Person Unicorn Thesis** — Sam Altman (OpenAI) revealed that his "groupchat with tech CEO friends" has **"a betting pool for the first year that there is a one-person billion-dollar company... that would have been unimaginable without AI and now will happen."** The share of new startups with a solo founder rose from 23.7% (2019) to 36.3% (first half 2025), per Carta. Over 44% of profitable SaaS businesses are now run by solo founders, many leveraging AI for >70% of development and marketing workflows.
- Source: [Entrepreneur Loop](https://entrepreneurloop.com/one-person-startup-billion-dollar-company-of-one/); [Stripe Indie Founder Report 2024]

**Midjourney** — Again, fewer than 15 people, $200M+ revenue, multi-billion-dollar valuation. A tiny human core orchestrating AI capabilities for millions of users.

### Coase's Theory Being Rewritten

**Thierry Warin (HEC Montreal / CIRANO)** — Published "From Coase to AI Agents: Why the Economics of the Firm Still Matters in the Age of Automation" in the *California Management Review* (April 2025). The argument: AI changes not just the microeconomics of transaction costs but the architecture of organizational coordination itself. However, **AI doesn't eliminate transaction costs — it transforms them.** New costs emerge around model selection, prompt engineering, output verification, and hallucination management. These serve the same economic function: determining organizational boundaries.
- Source: [California Management Review](https://cmr.berkeley.edu/2025/04/from-coase-to-ai-agents-why-the-economics-of-the-firm-still-matters-in-the-age-of-automation/)

**"The Coasean Singularity"** — An NBER working paper explores what happens when AI agents can handle market transactions: "When coordination costs approach zero, vertical integration loses its advantage. Why maintain a marketing department when you can access world-class AI capabilities on demand?" But the paper also identifies: **"Property rights ambiguity is the new transaction cost"** and **"Trust becomes the scarce resource — as functional costs approach zero, trust commands premium prices."**
- Source: [NBER](https://www.nber.org/system/files/chapters/c15309/c15309.pdf); [The Dangerous Economist](https://thedangerouseconomist.blogspot.com/2025/10/the-coasean-singularity-demand-supply.html)

**Winterspeak (Substack)** — "How will AI change the boundaries of the firm?" explores how AI lowers transaction costs for market coordination more than internal coordination, predicting a shift "from reliance on coordination using managerial hierarchies to using coordination by the market."
- Source: [Winterspeak on Substack](https://winterspeak.substack.com/p/how-will-ai-change-the-boundaries)

**Business History Perspective** — A July 2025 article at The Past Speaks applies business history to predict AI's impact on vertical integration, arguing the outcome is more nuanced than simple boundary collapse.
- Source: [The Past Speaks](https://pastspeaks.com/2025/07/07/the-likely-impact-of-ai-on-vertical-integration-insights-from-business-history/)

### Surprise / Counterintuitive Finding

The biggest surprise: **AI may not collapse the firm boundary — it may move it.** Warin's CMR paper argues that new transaction costs (hallucination management, prompt engineering, output verification, model selection) replace old ones. The firm doesn't disappear; it reconstitutes around *different* coordination problems. Organizations that tried to become pure orchestrators discovered they needed *more* internal capability to manage AI effectively, not less. Trust — the most human of capabilities — becomes the ultimate competitive advantage precisely when functional costs approach zero.

### How Might We...

- **HMW** help organizations identify which capabilities are core (must be internal) vs. which can be orchestrated externally in an AI-mediated world?
- **HMW** build trust as organizational infrastructure when the firm boundary becomes permeable?
- **HMW** manage the new transaction costs (hallucination management, output verification) that AI creates even as it eliminates old ones?

## 6. TRANSPARENCY VS. TRUST

**The tension:** AI makes work visible at unprecedented granularity. Visibility can empower or surveil — the difference is in design and intent.

### Transparency That Empowers

**Buffer (Joel Gascoigne, CEO)** — Since 2013, all employee salaries have been publicly available on Buffer's website, including employee name, team, role, location, and salary, with a detailed spreadsheet showing how each salary was calculated. This predates AI but demonstrates the principle: **radical transparency, when designed for equity rather than control, builds trust rather than eroding it.** Buffer's approach works because the transparency is *symmetrical* — everyone is visible, including leadership.
- Source: [Ravio](https://ravio.com/blog/compensation-examples-buffer)

**Microsoft Responsible AI Transparency Reports** — Microsoft publishes detailed transparency reports on how it builds and deploys AI, empowering employees and the public to understand system behavior. The approach: transparency about the *system* rather than surveillance of the *worker*.
- Source: [Microsoft](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/ai/principles-and-approach)

### Surveillance Dystopias

**Amazon's Algorithmic Management** — Amazon has built "a surveillance and enforcement stack designed not only to optimize logistics but to preempt solidarity, fracture trust, and atomize resistance." This includes monitoring 43+ Facebook groups (most nominally private), numerous subreddits, and Twitter keywords worldwide. Academic research from Teke Wiggin (2025) documented how Amazon "weaponized the workplace" through algorithmic management during the Bessemer, Alabama union campaign.
- Source: [The American Prospect](https://prospect.org/labor/2025-03-13-amazon-uses-arsenal-of-ai-weapons-against-workers/); [SAGE Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/23780231251318389)

**Microsoft Productivity Score (2020) — and the Sequel** — Microsoft's 2020 "Productivity Score" tracked individual employees: emails sent, chat frequency, document participation. Backlash forced Microsoft to remove individual names. In 2025, Microsoft introduced new Copilot usage benchmarks in Viva Insights that let managers see "how often teams use Copilot" — drawing immediate comparisons to the earlier controversy. The company built privacy safeguards (minimum 20-company peer groups), but the tension remains.
- Source: [WinBuzzer](https://winbuzzer.com/2025/10/23/microsofts-new-workplace-tracking-tools-spark-new-privacy-debate-xcxwbn/)

**Industry-Wide Statistics** — 80% of U.S. companies now track employee performance digitally. Eight in ten employees report that monitoring erodes trust. In the EU, 42.3% of workers are affected by algorithmic management.
- Source: [AI Now Institute](https://ainowinstitute.org/publications/algorithmic-management); [European Working Condition Survey 2024]

### The Distinction: Empowerment vs. Control

**AI Now Institute** — Published "Algorithmic Management: Restraining Workplace Surveillance," establishing a policy framework for distinguishing productive transparency from destructive surveillance.
- Source: [AI Now](https://ainowinstitute.org/publications/algorithmic-management)

**Van Zoonen, von Bonsdorff & van der Heijden (2025)** — Published in *Human Relations*: algorithmic surveillance erodes trust in governance, fostering resentment and resistance. "Algorithmic transparency further strengthens positive effects, accentuating the value of clear and accessible communication around algorithmic processes." The key finding: **transparency *about* algorithms builds trust; transparency *through* algorithms erodes it.**
- Source: [SAGE Journals](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00187267251379698)

**Race, Power & Policy** — A March 2026 analysis argues: "Whether AI exploits or benefits workers depends on whether the balance of power leans more in favor of labor or management." The suggestion: workers should have access to their own monitoring data, not just management.
- Source: [Race, Power & Policy](https://racepowerpolicy.org/2026/03/ai-surveillance-and-exploitation-are-pushing-down-labor-value/)

### Surprise / Counterintuitive Finding

The distinction between empowering transparency and controlling surveillance is not about *what* is made visible but **who controls the visibility and for whose benefit.** Buffer's radical salary transparency is empowering because it's symmetrical (everyone visible), voluntary (company chose it), and equity-oriented (designed to eliminate pay gaps). Amazon's monitoring is controlling because it's asymmetrical (workers visible to management, not vice versa), compulsory, and extraction-oriented (designed to maximize output). **Same mechanism (making work visible), opposite outcomes, depending entirely on power dynamics and design intent.**

### How Might We...

- **HMW** design AI visibility tools that are symmetrical — giving workers the same insight into organizational decisions that managers have into worker behavior?
- **HMW** create transparency standards that distinguish between empowering visibility and controlling surveillance?
- **HMW** ensure workers have ownership of (or at least access to) the data generated about their own work?

## 7. NEW VALUE VS. OPTIMIZATION

**The tension:** Most AI organizational changes are about efficiency. The real prize is using AI-driven org design to create things that couldn't exist before.

### Creating Genuinely New Value

**Recursion Pharmaceuticals / Insilico Medicine** — These AI-native pharma companies didn't just optimize drug discovery — they redesigned the *organization* to make previously impossible science possible. Recursion employs computational biologists, data engineers, ML specialists, and software developers in roughly equal numbers to wet lab scientists — a fundamentally different org structure. Insilico identified a novel target and designed a clinical candidate in 18 months (vs. 4-6 years traditionally). AI-designed molecules show 80-90% Phase I success rates vs. 40-65% for traditional discovery. **The organizational structure IS the innovation.**
- Source: [Recursion](https://www.recursion.com/mission); [ITONICS](https://www.itonics-innovation.com/blog/ai-native-pharma-startups)

**BCG's Agentic AI Value Creation Research (2025)** — BCG found that companies seeing the most value from AI "often set growth or innovation as additional objectives, beyond the efficiency focus that 80% of respondents prioritize." A global consumer goods company rebuilt product innovation around meta-agents orchestrating worker agents, cutting cycle time by ~60% while improving product fit. A shipbuilder cut engineering lead time by 60%. A telecom deployed AI assistants sending 40,000 messages daily, achieving a fivefold jump in digital sales.
- Source: [BCG](https://www.bcg.com/publications/2025/agents-accelerate-next-wave-of-ai-value-creation)

**McKinsey State of AI 2025** — "High-performing companies stand out for thinking beyond incremental efficiency gains: they treat AI as a catalyst to transform their organizations, redesigning workflows and accelerating innovation." The key distinction: **rewiring how companies run (workflow redesign) has the biggest effect on whether organizations see EBIT impact from AI.**
- Source: [McKinsey](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/quantumblack/our-insights/the-state-of-ai)

### Stuck in the Optimization Trap

**80% Default to Efficiency** — McKinsey's 2025 survey found that 80% of organizations using AI focus primarily on efficiency. Only the highest-performing organizations set growth or innovation as *additional* objectives. The optimization trap: AI makes efficiency gains so visible and measurable that they crowd out harder-to-quantify innovation investments.

**IMD AI Maturity Index 2025** — Found that many organizations pursue "practical applications" but fail to make the leap to "transformative change beyond incremental value." The gap is not in technology but in organizational ambition and structure.
- Source: [IMD](https://www.imd.org/ibyimd/white-papers/ai-strategies-that-are-working/)

### Organizational Structures That Enable Innovation

**The "Ambidextrous Organization" Revisited** — McKinsey suggests AI may enable truly ambidextrous organizations that "simultaneously optimize for efficiency in core operations while maintaining radically different structures for innovation work." The agentic organization model supports this: core operations run by AI agents optimizing for efficiency, while human-led teams focus on novel problems.

**Computational-Experimental Fusion** — The Recursion/Insilico model suggests a structural pattern for innovation: organizations built around the *integration* of AI and human capabilities from day one, rather than layering AI onto existing structures. "Traditional pharmaceutical companies can't match this without wholesale reorganization. Adding computational talent to existing structures creates service functions, not integrated teams."

### Surprise / Counterintuitive Finding

**The companies creating genuinely new value with AI are not the ones most people associate with "AI companies."** The most transformative AI-driven organizational redesigns are happening in biotech, not Big Tech. Recursion and Insilico have redesigned their entire organizations around AI-human fusion to create drugs that couldn't have been discovered otherwise. Meanwhile, most tech companies are using AI primarily for cost optimization (Klarna, Duolingo). **The industries least associated with AI culture are doing the most innovative organizational design with it.**

Also surprising: McKinsey found that workflow redesign — not AI tool adoption — is the biggest predictor of financial impact. **The organizational change matters more than the technology.**

### How Might We...

- **HMW** help leaders distinguish between AI projects that optimize existing value and AI projects that create genuinely new value — and resource both appropriately?
- **HMW** design organizational structures that make innovation the *default* use of AI rather than the exception?
- **HMW** apply the Recursion/Insilico model of "computational-experimental fusion" to industries beyond pharma?

## Cross-Cutting Themes

Several patterns emerge across all seven tensions:

1. **The Klarna Parabola** — Initial compression looks successful on every metric. The capability loss is a lagging indicator. This pattern repeats across multiple tensions.

2. **Military Wisdom** — The most hierarchical institution in society has the deepest thinking about coherent autonomy, speed with judgment, and distributed decision-making. Corporate AI transformation would benefit from studying mission command doctrine.

3. **HR as the Binding Constraint** — Across tensions 1, 4, and 6, HR systems (job descriptions, compensation, performance reviews, career ladders) emerge as the slowest-moving component and the binding constraint on transformation.

4. **Trust as the New Scarce Resource** — When AI makes functional costs approach zero, trust becomes the competitive advantage. This appears in the Coase discussion (Tension 5), the transparency discussion (Tension 6), and the Klarna reversal (Tension 1).

5. **Organizational Design IS the Innovation** — The most transformative uses of AI are not tool adoptions but organizational redesigns. Recursion's structure, Anthropic's RSP, and the military's adaptive command model are all cases where the organizational architecture is the primary innovation.

6. **The Pace Layers Imperative** — Organizations that accelerate their fast layers (shipping, coding, content) without investing in their slow layers (governance, culture, trust) consistently produce the worst outcomes across all seven tensions.
