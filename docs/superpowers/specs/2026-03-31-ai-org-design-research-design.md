# Research Design: Organizing for AI Leverage

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Author:** David Beath / BuildFirst
**Status:** Approved — ready for research execution

## Purpose

An internal research dossier for BuildFirst that serves as the intellectual foundation and stimulus material for an IDEO-style design sprint on AI-driven organizational redesign.

Sprint participants are diverse senior functional leaders tasked with extracting real leverage from AI. They are not org design theorists or AI experts — they are operators who need to act.

## Core Question

What are AI-native organizations doing that is structurally different, who is successfully translating those principles into incumbent organizations, and what are the unresolved tensions that every leader will have to navigate?

## What This Document Is Not

A literature review. A trend report. A "state of AI" survey. It is a provocation engine — designed to surface hard questions, not provide easy answers.

## Tone

Direct, concrete, opinionated where warranted. Named people and companies throughout. Written to be read aloud and argued with in a room.

## Document Structure

### Layer 1: The Landscape Shift (2-3 pages)

The specific structural shift that matters for org design: AI is not a productivity tool bolted onto existing structures — it is a coordination technology that changes the economics of organizing. When the cost of coordination drops, the optimal size, shape, and logic of an organization changes. Brief, sharp, grounded in 2-3 concrete examples.

### Layer 2: The Tensions (15-20 pages)

5-7 core tensions, each with identical structure:

- **The tension stated plainly** — one sentence, no jargon
- **The AI-native position** — who's on this side, named companies and people
- **The incumbent position** — who's navigating this from inside a larger org
- **The bridge** — who has successfully translated edge thinking into incumbent reality, and how (centerpiece of each tension)
- **The surprise** — something counterintuitive or uncomfortable
- **"How Might We..."** — 2-3 provocations for sprint participants

Candidate tensions (to be refined by research):

1. **Compression vs. Capability Building** — Running lean without hollowing out competitive capability
2. **Autonomy vs. Coherence** — Pushing decisions to the edge while holding strategic direction
3. **Speed vs. Judgment** — Moving fast without losing institutional wisdom
4. **Roles vs. Flows** — Fluid work design vs. HR systems built around fixed roles
5. **Internal vs. External** — Where the organization ends when AI collapses boundaries
6. **Transparency vs. Trust** — Radical visibility without surveillance
7. **New Value vs. Optimization** — Shifting attention from efficiency to genuine innovation

### Layer 3: The Leaders Map (5-7 pages)

Curated directory of people and organizations worth watching, organized by perspective:

- Structural innovators
- Value creators
- Bridge builders (edge-to-incumbent translators)
- Contrarians

Enough context per entry that a sprint participant could look them up and get value in 10 minutes.

### Appendix: Source Trail

Where the research went. Unconventional sources become valuable on their own — sprint participants will want to know where to keep learning.

## Research Sources

Deliberately unconventional — going beyond HBR/McKinsey/BCG:

- Founder and operator podcasts/interviews (including smaller shows)
- Substack and long-form blogs from practitioners (not consultants)
- Chinese tech commentary — translated primary sources and local analysts
- European startup ecosystem sources (Mistral, Granola, Poolside, etc.)
- Academic work outside business schools (complexity science, sociotechnical systems, military org design)
- Y Combinator and venture community (partner essays, batch patterns)
- Design and systems thinking communities
- Government and policy sources (labor economists, firm restructuring data)

## Research Targets

- Named founders who built AI-native organizations with distinctive structures
- Named leaders inside incumbents successfully bridging edge practices
- Named thinkers articulating new org design frameworks (heterodox, not mainstream consulting)
- Named contrarians with credible arguments against the prevailing narrative

## Deliverables

### Today (Layer 1)

Full research dossier covering all sections. Substantive but designed for iteration.

### Subsequent Layers (future sessions)

- Deeper case studies on bridge builders
- Primary source interview/outreach targets
- Sprint-ready stimulus cards (one-page provocations per tension)
- Geographic deep dives (China, Europe)

## Sprint Context

- **Format:** IDEO-like design sprint
- **Participants:** Deliberately diverse senior functional leaders
- **IDEO methods in play:** Analogous inspiration, extreme users, How Might We framing
- **Key emphasis:** The bridge — not just what the edge is doing, but who is successfully translating edge practices into incumbent organizations
